\frametitle{Brownian motion with drift}
A particle following Brownian motion with drift $\mu$ and volatility $\sigma$ satisfies
\be
  dx = \mu \, dt + \sigma \, dW\,.
\ee
The figure below shows three sample paths with different drift values.

\end{center}
}

\frame{
\frametitle{Ensemble averages}
For an ensemble of $N$ independent paths, the sample mean converges to $\mu t$.
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[height=6cm]{./figs/pdf/ensemble-paths.pdf}
\end{center}
}

\frame{
\frametitle{Key observations}
\bi
\item The \red{ensemble average} grows linearly: $\ave{x(t)} = \mu t$
\item Individual paths fluctuate around this mean
\item Higher $\sigma$ increases the spread of paths
\item The \red{time average} for a single path converges to $\mu t$ only as $t \to \infty$
\ei
\vf
This distinction between ensemble and time averages is central to ergodicity economics.
}
