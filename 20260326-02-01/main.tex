\frame{
\frametitle{Geometric Brownian Motion}
  Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) is the workhorse model of financial assets:
\be
  dx = x(\mu \, dt + \sigma \, dW\,)
\ee
with drift, $\mu$, and volatility, $\sigma$.

\vspace{1cm}
\begin{figure}[H]
\fbox{
  \parbox{7cm}{
  \vspace{0.2cm}\textbf{Multiplicative dynamics:} \\
  
  Ensemble average growth rate: $g_{\langle \rangle} = \mu$ \\
  Time average growth rate: $\bar{g}$ = $\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}$\\
  }
}
\end{figure}
}
%----------------------- 4 ----------------------

\frame{
\frametitle{Portfolio choice}
  Assume there are only two assets available
  \bi
    \item Risk-free asset:     
    \be 
    dx_r = x_r \, \mu_r \, dt 
    \ee
    \item Risky asset (stock):
    \be
        dx_s = x_s \, ( \mu_s \, dt + \sigma_s \, dW)
    \ee
        
  \ei
  \vspace{1cm}
  \textbf{Portfolio problem:} Allocate wealth to each of the two assets. \\
  Leverage, $\ell$, is the share of wealth allocated to the risky asset.
}

%----------------------- 5 ----------------------

\frame{
\frametitle{Optimal leverage}
  Portfolio dynamic:
  \be
    dx = x((\mu_r + \ell \,\mu_e )\, dt + \ell \, \sigma_s \, dW\,)
  \ee
  where  $\mu_e$ is the excess drift, $\mu_s - \mu_r$.

  The time average growth rate is
  \be
    \bar{g} = \mu_r + \ell \, \mu_e - \ell^2  \,\frac{\sigma_s^2}{2}
  \ee
  Maximising $\bar{g}$ with respect to $\ell$ gives us the
 \begin{figure}[H]
 \fbox{
  \parbox{6cm}{
  \textbf{Optimal leverage:} \\
  \be
    \ell_{opt} = \frac{\mu_e}{\sigma_s^2}
  \ee
  }
}
\end{figure}
}

%----------------------- 6 ----------------------

\frame{
\frametitle{Comparison to standard models}
  Portfolio choice is usually taught through
  \bi
    \item Markowitz portfolio theory:\\
    Choose a mix between the tangency portfolio and the risk-free asset.
    \item Sharpe ratio:\\
    Choose the portfolio with the highest Sharpe ratio: $\frac{\mu_s-\mu_r}{\sigma_s}$
  \ei
  Standard theory recognises the trade-off between risk and return but offers no guidance on how to do the trade-off
  (optimal choice depends on risk preferences).
  \vspace{1cm}
  
  Ergodicity Economics does!
}
